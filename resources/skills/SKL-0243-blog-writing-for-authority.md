## Core Concepts

A blog is the foundation of a personal brand's owned content. Ghost powers over 3 million publications because the model works: publish on a domain you control, optimize for search, and convert readers to subscribers. Unlike social media posts that decay in hours, blog posts compound in value over months and years through organic search traffic.

### The Pillar and Cluster Model

Organize your blog around 3-5 pillar topics that align with your expertise:

- **Pillar post** (2,000-3,000 words) — Comprehensive guide on a core topic. This is your definitive resource.
- **Cluster posts** (800-1,500 words) — Specific subtopics that link back to the pillar. Each answers one focused question.
- **Internal linking** — Every cluster post links to its pillar. Every pillar links to its clusters. This signals topic authority to search engines.

Example for a product management niche:
- Pillar: "The Complete Guide to Product Discovery"
- Clusters: "How to Run User Interviews," "Jobs-to-Be-Done Framework," "Opportunity Scoring Methods"

### Blog Post Structure

Follow this structure for every authority-building post:

1. **Headline** — Include the primary keyword. Use "How to," numbered lists, or specific outcomes. "How to Write API Documentation That Developers Actually Read" beats "API Documentation Tips."
2. **Opening hook** (2-3 sentences) — State the problem or gap. No lengthy preambles.
3. **Promise** (1 sentence) — Tell the reader exactly what they will learn.
4. **Body sections** — Use H2 headings for major sections, H3 for subsections. One idea per section. Include examples, code snippets, screenshots, or frameworks.
5. **Summary** — Restate the 3-5 key points.
6. **CTA** — Direct readers to a related content upgrade, newsletter signup, or next post.

### SEO Fundamentals for Authority Blogs

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| Title tag | Primary keyword near the beginning, under 60 characters |
| Meta description | Compelling summary, 150-160 characters, includes keyword |
| URL slug | Short, descriptive, keyword-included (`/product-discovery-guide`) |
| Headings | H2s contain secondary keywords naturally |
| Images | Descriptive alt text, compressed file sizes |
| Internal links | 3-5 links to related posts on your blog |
| External links | 2-3 links to authoritative sources (studies, documentation) |

### Content Upgrades

A content upgrade is a bonus resource offered within a blog post in exchange for an email address. It converts readers into subscribers at 5-15x the rate of a generic sidebar signup form.

Effective content upgrades by post type:

| Post Type | Content Upgrade | Format |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| How-to guide | Checklist or template | PDF |
| Framework post | Fillable worksheet | Spreadsheet |
| Tool comparison | Decision matrix | PDF or Notion template |
| Case study | Full dataset or slides | Download link |

Rules: The content upgrade must be directly relevant to the post topic. Generic "Subscribe to my newsletter" is not a content upgrade.

### Publishing Cadence

Quality over quantity. One well-researched post per week builds authority faster than daily thin content.

- **Minimum viable cadence** — 2 posts per month
- **Growth cadence** — 1 post per week
- **Refresh cycle** — Update your top 5 posts every 6 months with new data and examples

## Key Takeaways

- Blog content compounds through search traffic; social posts decay in hours
- Use the pillar and cluster model to build topic authority with 3-5 core themes
- Every post follows: headline, hook, promise, structured body, summary, CTA
- Content upgrades convert readers to subscribers at 5-15x the rate of generic signups
- Publish consistently at a sustainable cadence and refresh top posts every 6 months
