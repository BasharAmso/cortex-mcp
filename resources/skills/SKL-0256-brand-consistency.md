## Core Concepts

Ghost's theme system demonstrates why brand consistency matters technically: a single theme controls how all content appears across your publication, ensuring every post, page, and newsletter looks like it belongs to the same brand. Your personal brand needs the same architecture. Define once, apply everywhere.

### The Brand Consistency Triangle

Three elements must align across every touchpoint:

1. **Visual Identity** — Colors, typography, imagery style, logo usage
2. **Voice & Messaging** — How you sound, what you talk about, your perspective
3. **Experience** — How it feels to interact with you (response time, quality, tone)

If any one element contradicts the others, trust erodes. A polished website with sloppy social media replies signals inauthenticity.

### Visual Identity Minimum Viable Kit

You don't need a full brand book. Start with these 5 decisions:

| Element | Decision | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Primary color** | One dominant color for headers, buttons, accents | #2563EB (blue) |
| **Secondary color** | One supporting color for backgrounds, subtle elements | #F8FAFC (light gray) |
| **Heading font** | One font for titles (bold, distinctive) | Inter Bold |
| **Body font** | One font for paragraphs (readable, clean) | Inter Regular |
| **Photo style** | Consistent treatment for all images | Bright, natural light, minimal filters |

Use the same profile photo across all platforms. Update it once a year at most. Recognition requires repetition.

### Messaging Framework

Define these three statements and reference them whenever you create content:

1. **Positioning statement** — "I help [audience] achieve [outcome] through [method]." This is your LinkedIn headline, Twitter bio, and website hero text with minor variations.
2. **Three pillars** — The 3 topics you are known for. Everything you publish should connect to one of these. Ghost's Content API enables multi-channel distribution from a single source of truth; your pillars serve the same function for your messaging.
3. **Point of view** — What do you believe that others in your space don't? This is your differentiator. "AI won't replace product managers, but PMs who use AI will replace those who don't" is a point of view. "AI is changing everything" is not.

### Cross-Platform Audit Checklist

Run this audit quarterly:

- [ ] Profile photos match across LinkedIn, Twitter/X, YouTube, website, email signature
- [ ] Bio/headline communicates the same positioning on every platform
- [ ] Colors and fonts are consistent on website, presentations, social media graphics
- [ ] Tone of voice is recognizably the same person across platforms
- [ ] Links between platforms are working and up to date
- [ ] Content pillars are reflected in recent posts (not drifting into random topics)

### Common Consistency Killers

- **Platform mimicry** — Sounding corporate on LinkedIn and casual on Twitter creates a split personality. Adapt format (short vs. long), but keep voice constant.
- **Template overload** — Using different Canva templates for every post. Pick 2-3 templates and rotate.
- **Inconsistent publishing** — Disappearing for weeks then flooding feeds. A steady cadence builds recognition.

## Key Takeaways

- Align visual identity, voice, and experience across every platform
- Start with 5 visual decisions (2 colors, 2 fonts, photo style) and apply everywhere
- Define a positioning statement, 3 content pillars, and a distinctive point of view
- Run a cross-platform audit quarterly to catch drift
- Adapt format to each platform but keep your voice constant
