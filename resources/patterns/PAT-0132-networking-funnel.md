## Core Concepts

Cal.com's booking flow models the ideal networking pipeline: strangers become scheduled meetings through a structured, low-friction process. Just as Cal.com moves someone from a booking link to a confirmed calendar event through clear steps, your networking should move people through defined stages from initial contact to active collaboration.

### The Connection-to-Collaboration Pipeline

Networking is not collecting contacts. It is a funnel with intentional stages:

```
Discovery → Engagement → Conversation → Value Exchange → Collaboration
```

**Stage 1: Discovery** — Find the right people. Quality over quantity.
- Follow 20-30 people in your niche on LinkedIn/Twitter
- Attend 1-2 virtual or in-person events per month
- Join 2-3 communities where your target connections participate

**Stage 2: Engagement** — Be visible before you reach out.
- Comment thoughtfully on their content for 2-4 weeks
- Share their work with your audience and tag them
- React to their posts with substance, not just "Great post!"

**Stage 3: Conversation** — Move to direct communication.
- Send a short DM referencing something specific they said or wrote
- Ask a genuine question, not a favor. "Your take on X was interesting. How did you come to that conclusion?"
- Offer something small with no strings: a relevant article, an introduction, feedback on their project

**Stage 4: Value Exchange** — Create mutual benefit.
- Suggest a 20-minute virtual coffee. Cal.com-style booking links reduce scheduling friction. Send your availability, not an open-ended "when are you free?"
- Come prepared with one specific way you might help them
- Follow up within 24 hours with a short note and any promised resources

**Stage 5: Collaboration** — Work together on something.
- Co-create content (podcast guest swap, co-authored article, joint webinar)
- Make introductions to people in your network who could help them
- Propose a small project before suggesting large commitments

### Outreach Message Templates

**First DM (after 2-4 weeks of engagement):**
> "Hey [Name], I've been following your posts on [topic] and your point about [specific insight] really stuck with me. I'm working on something similar. Would love to hear more about your approach if you're open to a quick chat."

**Booking request (after positive DM exchange):**
> "Would a 20-minute call work? Here's my booking link: [cal.com link]. Pick any time that works for you. Happy to work around your schedule."

**Follow-up (within 24 hours of call):**
> "Really enjoyed our conversation about [topic]. As promised, here's [resource/intro/link]. Let me know if [specific offer to help] would be useful."

### The 5-5-5 Weekly Routine

Spend 25 minutes per week on intentional networking:

| Action | Time | Frequency |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Comment thoughtfully on 5 posts | 10 min | Weekly |
| Send 5 DMs to warm connections | 10 min | Weekly |
| Schedule or attend 1 conversation | 5 min to schedule | Weekly |

This cadence builds 250+ meaningful interactions per year without consuming your schedule.

### Common Mistakes

- **Pitching on first contact** — Never ask for something in your first message. Give value first, always.
- **Mass outreach** — Personalized messages to 5 people outperform generic messages to 50.
- **Neglecting follow-up** — One conversation means nothing without follow-through. Set a reminder to check in every 4-6 weeks.
- **Only networking up** — Connecting with peers and people earlier in their journey builds a broader, more reciprocal network than only targeting "bigger" names.

## Key Takeaways

- Treat networking as a pipeline: Discovery, Engagement, Conversation, Value Exchange, Collaboration
- Engage with someone's content for 2-4 weeks before sending a direct message
- Use booking links to reduce scheduling friction when moving to calls
- Follow the 5-5-5 weekly routine: 5 comments, 5 DMs, 1 conversation
- Always lead with value, never pitch on first contact
