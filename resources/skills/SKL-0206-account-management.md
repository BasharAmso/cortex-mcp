## Core Concepts

Acquiring a new customer costs 5-7x more than retaining an existing one. Account management is the discipline of growing revenue from your current customer base through deliberate health monitoring, relationship building, and expansion plays.

### Account Health Scoring

Build a composite health score from leading indicators rather than waiting for lagging ones like churn notices. Key inputs:

| Signal | Weight | What It Tells You |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Product usage frequency | High | Are they getting value? |
| Support ticket volume/sentiment | Medium | Are they frustrated? |
| Stakeholder engagement | High | Do champions still respond? |
| Contract renewal timeline | Medium | Is urgency building? |
| Payment behavior | Low | Are they financially healthy? |

Platforms like erxes demonstrate the value of centralizing contact data, segmentation, and automation into a single view. Build your health dashboard from CRM data, product analytics, and support systems combined.

### The Expansion Playbook

Upselling works when it solves a real problem, not when it adds features the customer does not need. Three reliable expansion triggers:

1. **Usage ceiling** -- the customer is hitting plan limits or requesting capacity increases
2. **New use case** -- a different team or department starts asking about your product
3. **Business event** -- funding round, acquisition, or headcount growth creates new needs

Time your expansion conversations to coincide with these triggers, not arbitrary quarterly quotas.

### Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs)

Run structured reviews with each strategic account. A QBR should cover: value delivered since last review, usage trends, open issues, upcoming roadmap items relevant to them, and mutual goals for next quarter. QBRs maintain executive sponsorship and surface expansion opportunities before competitors do.

### Stakeholder Mapping

Track the decision-maker, economic buyer, champion, and end users for each account. When your champion leaves, your account is at risk. Erxes-style omnichannel engagement (conversations, tickets, tasks in one view) helps maintain multiple relationship threads so the account does not depend on a single contact.

### Churn Prevention

Watch for early warning signals: declining login frequency, unresolved escalations, leadership changes, or silence from previously engaged contacts. Intervene with a direct conversation, not an automated email. Personal outreach from the account owner when health scores dip is the most effective save mechanism.

## Key Takeaways

- Build health scores from leading indicators like usage and engagement, not just renewal dates
- Time expansion conversations to natural triggers, not arbitrary quotas
- Run structured QBRs to maintain executive relationships and surface opportunities
- Map multiple stakeholders per account to reduce single-point-of-failure risk
- Intervene personally at the first sign of declining health, not after the churn notice
