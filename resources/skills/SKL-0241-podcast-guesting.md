## Core Concepts

Podcast guesting is the highest-trust personal brand channel. Listeners hear your voice for 30-60 minutes, creating parasocial connection that no written post can match. Ghost's creator ecosystem shows that the most successful publishers combine written content with audio appearances to reach audiences in different contexts. The strategy is not to appear on the biggest shows but to appear consistently on the right ones.

### Finding the Right Shows

Target shows where your expertise solves a problem for the host's audience:

| Show Size | Listeners/Episode | Booking Difficulty | Value |
|-----------|------------------|--------------------|-------|
| **Micro** (under 500) | 100-500 | Easy | High per-listener conversion, hosts are grateful |
| **Small** (500-5K) | 500-5,000 | Moderate | Sweet spot for authority building |
| **Medium** (5K-50K) | 5,000-50,000 | Competitive | Significant reach, requires strong pitch |
| **Large** (50K+) | 50,000+ | Very hard | Save for when you have proven talking points |

Start with micro and small shows. Hosts are more responsive, you get reps refining your message, and episodes rank in search for years.

### The Guest Pitch Template

Keep your pitch under 150 words. Hosts receive dozens of pitches weekly.

**Structure:**
1. **Compliment** (1 sentence) — Reference a specific episode and what you took from it
2. **Relevance** (1-2 sentences) — Why your expertise matters to their audience
3. **Topics** (3 bullet points) — Specific episode ideas, not vague themes
4. **Credibility** (1 sentence) — Your most relevant accomplishment or experience
5. **Easy out** (1 sentence) — "No worries if the timing isn't right"

What kills pitches: generic templates, no evidence you have listened to the show, topic ideas that are too broad ("I'd love to talk about leadership").

### Interview Preparation

Prepare three elements before any appearance:

1. **3 signature stories** — Personal, specific anecdotes that illustrate your key points. Stories are remembered; advice is forgotten.
2. **Quotable sound bites** — 1-2 sentence versions of your best insights. Hosts love guests who deliver clip-worthy moments.
3. **The bridge** — A practiced pivot from any question back to your core expertise. "That's a great point, and it connects to something I see a lot in..."

### Maximizing Post-Appearance Value

A podcast episode is raw material, not a finished product:

- **Repurpose clips** — Pull 3-5 short clips (60-90 seconds) for social media
- **Write a companion post** — Expand on one topic from the interview as a blog post or LinkedIn post
- **Share strategically** — Tag the host, thank them publicly, share with your newsletter
- **Link from your profile** — Add notable appearances to your LinkedIn Featured section
- **Pitch related shows** — Reference the appearance when pitching similar podcasts ("I recently discussed X on [Show Name]")

### Guest Profile / One-Sheet

Create a simple one-page document to send hosts:

- Professional headshot (high-resolution)
- Bio (50 words and 150 words versions)
- 3-5 topic areas with specific episode title suggestions
- Links to previous appearances (if any)
- Social links and website

## Key Takeaways

- Start with micro and small shows to refine your message and build a track record
- Pitches must reference a specific episode and propose concrete topics, not vague themes
- Prepare signature stories, sound bites, and a bridge technique before every interview
- Repurpose every appearance into social clips, companion posts, and newsletter content
- A simple one-sheet guest profile makes it easy for hosts to say yes
