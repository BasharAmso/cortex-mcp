## Core Concepts

Your LinkedIn profile is a landing page, not a resume. Ghost's publishing philosophy applies directly: clear positioning, strong headlines, and structured content that guides readers toward a single action. Every section should answer the visitor's question: "Can this person help me?"

### Headline Formula

Your headline gets 120 characters of prime real estate. Use this structure:

**[Role] | [Who you help] + [How you help them]**

Examples:
- "Product Manager | Helping B2B teams ship AI features that users actually adopt"
- "Frontend Engineer | Building accessible design systems for startups"

Avoid title-only headlines ("Senior Developer at Company X"). They describe your employer, not your value.

### About Section Structure

Write your About section in three blocks, each 2-3 sentences:

1. **Hook** — Open with the problem you solve or the mission you care about. Lead with the reader's pain, not your credentials.
2. **Proof** — Share 2-3 specific results, projects, or experiences. Numbers and specifics beat adjectives.
3. **Call to action** — Tell people what to do next. "DM me about X" or "Check out my newsletter on Y" gives visitors a clear path.

Write in first person. Short paragraphs. No walls of text.

### Featured Section Curation

Your Featured section is your portfolio above the fold. Pin 3-5 items maximum:

- Your best-performing post (social proof via engagement)
- A link to your newsletter, blog, or project (owned platform)
- A case study, talk, or article that demonstrates expertise
- A lead magnet or free resource (audience capture)

Rotate Featured items monthly. Remove anything older than 6 months unless it is evergreen.

### Profile Completeness Checklist

| Element | Priority | Note |
|---------|----------|------|
| Professional headshot | Critical | Face clearly visible, simple background |
| Custom banner image | High | Reinforce your positioning or brand |
| Headline with value prop | Critical | Not just your job title |
| About section (3 blocks) | Critical | Hook, proof, CTA |
| Featured section (3-5 items) | High | Best work, owned platforms |
| Experience with descriptions | Medium | Impact statements, not job descriptions |
| Skills (top 3 pinned) | Medium | Match your headline positioning |

## Key Takeaways

- Treat your profile as a landing page optimized for one audience, not a resume for everyone
- Headlines should communicate who you help and how, not just your title
- The About section follows Hook, Proof, CTA structure in first person
- Featured section is your portfolio: curate aggressively, rotate monthly
- Every element should reinforce the same positioning message
