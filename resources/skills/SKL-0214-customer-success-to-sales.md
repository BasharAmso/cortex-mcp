## Core Concepts

Expansion revenue from existing customers is more efficient than new-logo acquisition. The cost of expanding an existing account is roughly one-fifth the cost of acquiring a new one. Customer success teams sit on a goldmine of buying signals, but the handoff to sales is where most organizations lose the opportunity.

### The CS-to-Sales Signal Chain

Erxes and similar experience operating systems unify customer interactions across support, sales, and marketing. This unification reveals expansion signals that siloed tools miss:

1. **Usage signals** - A customer consistently hitting plan limits, adding users, or adopting new features is showing expansion readiness.
2. **Support signals** - Questions about advanced features, integrations, or enterprise capabilities indicate growing needs.
3. **Sentiment signals** - High NPS scores (9-10) combined with high usage is the ideal expansion profile. Low NPS with high usage is a churn risk that needs intervention first.
4. **Lifecycle signals** - Contract renewal windows, anniversaries, and business planning seasons are natural expansion conversation points.

### NPS-Driven Upsell Framework

Segment your customer base into a 2x2 matrix:

| | High NPS (9-10) | Low NPS (0-6) |
|---|---|---|
| **High Usage** | Expansion target | Churn risk - fix first |
| **Low Usage** | Adoption risk - activate first | Immediate intervention |

Only pursue upsell conversations with customers in the high-NPS, high-usage quadrant. For the other three quadrants, the priority is retention and adoption, not revenue.

### Handoff Design

The CS-to-sales handoff must feel seamless to the customer. They should not feel "sold to" after building trust with their success manager. Best practices from the erxes model:

- **Warm introduction**: the CSM introduces the account executive in context ("I've asked Sarah to walk you through our enterprise features since you mentioned scaling to 500 users").
- **Shared context**: both CS and sales see the same customer record, including support history, usage data, and past conversations.
- **Joint meetings**: for strategic accounts, CS and sales attend expansion calls together. The CSM provides credibility; the AE handles commercial terms.
- **Attribution**: give CS credit (through comp or recognition) for expansion opportunities they source. If CS gets nothing for surfacing deals, they will stop surfacing them.

### Churn Prevention as Revenue Protection

Every dollar of prevented churn is a dollar of revenue. Build churn prevention playbooks triggered by health score drops: declining usage, support ticket spikes, missed QBRs (quarterly business reviews), or champion departure. The first response should always come from CS, not sales. Showing up with a discount when a customer is frustrated makes the problem worse.

## Key Takeaways

- Expansion revenue costs one-fifth of new-logo acquisition; treat existing customers as your best pipeline source.
- Use a 2x2 matrix of NPS and usage to prioritize which accounts to expand vs. which to stabilize first.
- Design warm CS-to-sales handoffs that carry full context so the customer never feels "handed off."
- Compensate customer success for sourcing expansion opportunities or the signal flow will dry up.
- Address churn risks with success interventions first; leading with discounts signals desperation.
