## Core Concepts

Ghost's publishing philosophy applies to media outreach: own your narrative. Ghost enables creators to publish independently rather than depending on platforms they don't control. Media outreach works the same way. Earned media amplifies your owned content; it should never be your only distribution channel.

### The Pitch Framework

Journalists receive hundreds of pitches daily. Yours needs to pass the "why should my readers care" test in the first two sentences.

**Pitch structure (5 lines max):**

1. **Hook** — A timely, specific claim tied to a trend or data point. "AI is changing everything" fails. "73% of enterprise PMs now use AI for sprint planning, up from 12% two years ago" works.
2. **Proof** — Why you are credible on this topic. One sentence with your most relevant credential or experience.
3. **Offer** — What you can provide: an expert quote, a case study, data, or an exclusive story.
4. **Format** — What you are proposing: a guest article, an interview, a contributed quote for their existing piece.
5. **Easy out** — "If this isn't a fit, no worries. Happy to be a resource for future pieces on [topic]."

### Building a Media List

Do not blast 500 journalists with the same email. Build a targeted list of 20-30 writers who actually cover your topic.

1. **Read their recent work** — Search their byline on the publication. If they haven't written about your topic in the last 6 months, skip them.
2. **Follow first** — Engage with their content on social media for 2-4 weeks before pitching. Journalists notice who reads their work.
3. **Use HARO/Qwoted/Help a B2B Writer** — Respond to journalist queries where they are actively looking for sources. This inverts the dynamic: they come to you.
4. **Track in a simple spreadsheet** — Name, outlet, beat, last article, date pitched, response. No CRM needed at small scale.

### Press Release vs. Pitch

| Situation | Use a Press Release | Use a Pitch |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Funding announcement | Yes | Follow up with pitch |
| Product launch | Yes (for SEO/archive) | Pitch specific angles to specific writers |
| Thought leadership | No | Yes, always |
| Speaking/award | No | Only if newsworthy to their audience |

Press releases are for the record. Pitches are for relationships. Most personal brand media coverage comes from pitches, not press releases.

### Timing Matters

- **Tuesday-Thursday mornings** have the highest open rates for pitch emails
- **Avoid Mondays** (inbox overload) and **Fridays** (weekend mode)
- **Tie to news cycles** — Pitch when a related story is trending. "I noticed your piece on X. Here's a different angle..."
- **Lead time** — Monthly magazines need 2-3 months. Online publications need 1-2 weeks. Breaking news needs hours.

## Key Takeaways

- Keep pitches to 5 lines or fewer with a specific, timely hook
- Build a targeted list of 20-30 journalists who cover your exact topic
- Engage with journalists' work before pitching them cold
- Use journalist query platforms (HARO, Qwoted) to get inbound media requests
- Treat press releases as records and pitches as relationship-builders
