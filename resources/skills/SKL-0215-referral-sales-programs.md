## Core Concepts

Referral sales programs turn happy customers into a scalable acquisition channel. Referred customers convert at higher rates, have lower acquisition costs, and tend to retain longer because they arrive with built-in trust from the referrer.

### Program Types

There are three common referral models, each suited to different business contexts:

1. **Customer referral** - Existing customers refer friends or colleagues in exchange for rewards (credits, discounts, cash). Best for B2C and PLG (product-led growth) companies.
2. **Partner/affiliate** - External partners (consultants, agencies, influencers) refer prospects for commissions. Best for B2B with complex sales where trusted advisors influence buying decisions.
3. **Employee referral** - Internal team members refer prospects from their professional networks. Often underused but high-trust.

### Incentive Design

The incentive must be valuable enough to motivate action but not so large that it attracts low-quality referrals. Proven structures:

- **Double-sided rewards**: Both the referrer and the referee get something. This removes the awkwardness of "selling" to a friend by framing it as sharing a benefit.
- **Tiered rewards**: Increase the reward as referrers hit milestones (3 referrals, 10 referrals). This gamifies the program and identifies your most active advocates.
- **Reward timing**: Pay rewards after the referred customer completes a qualifying action (first purchase, subscription activation), not just on signup. This ensures quality.

### Automation with Marketing Platforms

Mautic and similar marketing automation tools provide the infrastructure for referral programs:

- **Unique referral links**: Generate tracked URLs per referrer so attribution is automatic.
- **Drip campaigns**: When a referral signs up, trigger a personalized onboarding sequence that acknowledges the referral source ("Welcome! Sarah thought you'd love this").
- **Reward fulfillment**: Automate reward delivery when the qualifying action is confirmed, reducing manual ops overhead.
- **Referrer nurture**: Send periodic reminders to active referrers showing their impact ("You've referred 5 people this quarter, earning $250 in credits").

### Tracking and Attribution

Referral attribution must be bulletproof or the program loses credibility. Track via:
- **Referral codes**: unique per referrer, entered at signup.
- **Tracked links**: UTM-tagged URLs that auto-attribute in your CRM.
- **Cookie/fingerprint**: for cases where the prospect does not use the direct link.

Set a clear attribution window (e.g., 30 or 90 days) and communicate it upfront. Disputes over who gets credit kill program momentum.

### Measuring Program Health

Track **referral rate** (% of customers who refer), **conversion rate** (% of referred leads who become customers), **CAC comparison** (referral CAC vs. other channels), and **program ROI** (reward costs vs. revenue from referred customers).

## Key Takeaways

- Double-sided rewards remove the social friction of asking friends to buy something.
- Pay rewards on qualifying actions, not signups, to maintain lead quality.
- Automate referral tracking and reward fulfillment so the program scales without manual ops.
- Set clear attribution windows and communicate them to avoid disputes that erode trust.
- Measure referral CAC against other channels to prove the program's value to leadership.
