## Core Concepts

Effective negotiation is not about winning at the other side's expense. It is about creating agreements where both parties walk away with enough value to sustain the relationship. The best sales negotiations are built on preparation, not persuasion.

### BATNA: Your Walk-Away Power

BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement) is the single most important concept in negotiation. Before any conversation, define what you will do if this deal falls through. A strong BATNA gives you confidence to hold firm. A weak one means you need to strengthen your position before entering the room. Never negotiate without knowing your floor.

### Anchoring: Frame the Starting Point

The first number on the table shapes the entire conversation. Present your price or terms first when you have market data to justify them. Anchor high but credibly, backing your number with value delivered, comparable deals, or ROI projections. If the buyer anchors first, resist the pull by reframing around value rather than countering with a number.

### Concession Strategy

Never give something without getting something back. Plan your concessions in advance: know what you can trade (payment terms, scope, timeline) and what is non-negotiable (margin floor, core deliverables). Make concessions smaller over time to signal you are approaching your limit. The 37signals principle applies here: fixed constraints force clarity. Set your non-negotiables early, then negotiate within the remaining space.

### Win-Win Structure

Structure deals so both parties have skin in the game. Performance-based pricing, phased commitments, and pilot programs all create shared risk. When the buyer succeeds, you succeed. This alignment converts one-time transactions into long-term accounts.

### Trust as Negotiation Currency

Starting from a position of trust and cooperation, as 37signals advocates in their operational philosophy, produces faster agreements with less friction. Transparent pricing, honest timelines, and proactive communication about limitations build credibility that compounds over multiple deals.

## Key Takeaways

- Define your BATNA before every negotiation; it determines your confidence and floor
- Anchor with credible, value-backed numbers rather than arbitrary high bids
- Trade concessions strategically; never give without getting
- Structure deals for mutual success to convert transactions into partnerships
- Trust and transparency accelerate agreements more than aggressive tactics
