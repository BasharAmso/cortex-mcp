## Core Concepts

Your personal website is the only platform you fully control. Social media profiles can change algorithms, get suspended, or lose relevance. A personal site is your permanent home base on the internet. It does not need to be complex, but it does need to exist.

### Domain Selection

Choose a domain that is easy to spell, easy to remember, and ideally includes your name. FirstnameLastname.com is the gold standard. If that is taken, try variations: first initial + last name, your name + a relevant keyword (e.g., basharcodes.com), or a .dev/.io/.me extension. Buy the domain even if you are not ready to build yet. Domains cost roughly $12 per year and the best ones disappear fast.

### Essential Pages

A personal website needs exactly four pages to start. More can come later, but these four cover 90% of visitor needs:

1. **Home**: One clear sentence about who you are and what you do. A professional photo. One primary call-to-action (hire me, read my writing, book a call).
2. **About**: Your story in 200 to 300 words. What you do, why you do it, and what makes your perspective unique. Write in first person.
3. **Work/Portfolio**: Three to five curated projects with case study format (see SKL-0245). Link to live work where possible.
4. **Contact**: A simple form or direct email link. Include relevant social profiles. Remove friction.

### Platform Choice

Ghost is a strong option for professionals who plan to publish content alongside their portfolio. It offers clean design, built-in SEO, membership and newsletter features, and a headless CMS API for custom front-ends. For developers who want full control, a static site generator (Next.js, Astro, Hugo) deployed to Vercel or Netlify works well. For non-technical professionals, managed platforms like Squarespace or Carrd offer fast setup. The best platform is the one you will actually maintain.

### SEO Fundamentals

Search engine optimization for personal sites comes down to four basics. First, include your name and professional keywords in your page titles and meta descriptions. Second, write a blog or publish content regularly so search engines have fresh pages to index. Ghost handles technical SEO out of the box: clean URLs, structured data, XML sitemaps, and canonical tags. Third, earn backlinks by guest posting, getting mentioned in directories, or contributing to open-source projects. Fourth, ensure your site loads fast: compress images, use a CDN, and minimize JavaScript.

### Design Principles

Keep it simple. White space is your friend. Use one typeface for headings and one for body text. Stick to two or three brand colors. Ensure the site works well on mobile since over half of web traffic is mobile. Ghost's default themes demonstrate this well: minimal, readable, and focused on content rather than decoration.

## Key Takeaways

- Buy your domain name now, even if you are not ready to build. FirstnameLastname.com is ideal.
- Start with four pages: Home, About, Work, and Contact. Expand only when needed.
- Choose a platform you will actually maintain. Ghost, static generators, or managed hosts all work.
- Handle SEO basics: keywords in titles, regular content, fast load times, and backlinks.
- Prioritize simplicity and mobile readability over visual complexity.
