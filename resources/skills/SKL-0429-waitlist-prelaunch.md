## Core Concepts

A pre-launch strategy validates demand and builds momentum before you write a line of product code. The waitlist is not just an email list; it is your first product experiment.

### Landing Page Essentials

Your pre-launch page has one job: convert visitors into signups. Every element serves that goal.

| Element | Purpose | Priority |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Headline** | Communicate the value in one sentence | Must have |
| **Subheadline** | Explain who it is for and why it matters | Must have |
| **Email capture** | Single field + CTA button | Must have |
| **Social proof** | "Join 500+ others" or founder credibility | High |
| **Visual/screenshot** | Show the product concept (mockup is fine) | High |
| **FAQ section** | Preempt objections | Nice to have |

### Waitlist with Viral Mechanics

A referral loop turns each signup into a distribution channel:

1. User signs up with their email
2. They receive a unique referral link and their position in line
3. Each referral moves them up the queue (or unlocks rewards)
4. Reward tiers create urgency: 3 referrals = early access, 10 = lifetime discount

This mechanic powered launches for Robinhood (1M+ waitlist), Superhuman, and Linear.

### Pre-Launch Content Strategy

Do not go silent between "page live" and "product ready." Build anticipation:

- **Week 1-2:** Share the problem you are solving (blog post, social media)
- **Week 3-4:** Behind-the-scenes updates (building in public)
- **Week 5-6:** Sneak peek screenshots or demo videos
- **Launch week:** Coordinated announcement across all channels

### Measuring Pre-Launch Success

| Metric | Good Benchmark |
|--------|---------------|
| Landing page conversion | 20-40% of visitors sign up |
| Viral coefficient | > 0.5 (each signup brings 0.5 more) |
| Email open rate | > 40% for update emails |
| Referral participation | > 15% of signups share their link |

### Tools for Quick Setup

You do not need a custom-built waitlist. Start with:
- **Landing page:** Carrd, Framer, or a single Next.js page
- **Email capture:** ConvertKit, Buttondown, or Resend
- **Referral tracking:** Waitlist.me, Viral Loops, or a simple database counter

## Key Takeaways
- Your landing page has one job: capture emails with a clear value proposition
- Referral mechanics turn each signup into a distribution channel
- Do not go silent between launch page and product launch; build in public
- A 20-40% landing page conversion rate validates demand
- Start with existing tools; build custom only when you outgrow them
