# Content Idea Capture System

Never start from blank. Build a system that captures ideas faster than you use them. Like Book Genesis's research phase that feeds the entire pipeline, idea capture is the foundation of sustainable content.

## The Idea Bank

A single place where every content idea goes the moment it occurs. The tool doesn't matter. The habit does.

| Tool | Best For | Capture Speed |
|------|----------|--------------|
| **Apple/Google Notes** | Quick text on mobile | Fastest |
| **Voice Memos** | Ideas while walking/driving | Fast |
| **Notion database** | Structured idea management | Medium |
| **Dedicated Slack channel** | Team idea sharing | Medium |
| **Email to self** | Universal fallback | Fast |

**Rule: Capture in under 30 seconds.** If it takes longer, you won't do it consistently.

## What to Capture

Write just enough to reconstruct the idea later:
- One-line hook or headline
- Why it matters (one sentence)
- Source trigger (conversation, article, experience, question)

## Idea Sources

| Source | What to Capture |
|--------|----------------|
| **Comments on your posts** | Questions = content they want |
| **DMs and emails** | Repeated questions = proven demand |
| **Competitor content** | Topics getting engagement (add your angle) |
| **Your own experience** | Lessons, mistakes, wins, processes |
| **Industry news** | Your reaction or take |
| **Books and podcasts** | Key insights applied to your niche |
| **Client conversations** | Pain points, objections, aha moments |

## Idea Scoring Framework

When you have 20+ ideas, score on three dimensions:

| Dimension | Question | Score 1-5 |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Relevance** | Does my audience care right now? | _ |
| **Uniqueness** | Can I say something different? | _ |
| **Timeliness** | Is there a reason to post now? | _ |

**Total = Relevance x Uniqueness x Timeliness** (max 125)
- 60+: Create this week
- 30-59: Strong candidate, schedule it
- Under 30: Keep in bank, revisit later

## Idea to Outline (5-Minute Process)

1. Write the hook (first line that stops scrolling)
2. List 3-5 key points (one sentence each)
3. Write the CTA (what should the reader do?)
4. Note the format (story, list, how-to, opinion)

This takes 5 minutes and makes the writing session 3x faster.

## Weekly Maintenance

| Action | Frequency | Time |
|--------|-----------|------|
| Dump new ideas into the bank | Daily | 2 min |
| Review and score top ideas | Weekly | 15 min |
| Turn top 5 into outlines | Weekly | 25 min |
| Archive stale ideas (3+ months, low score) | Monthly | 10 min |

## Anti-Patterns

- Using your brain as the idea bank (ideas vanish within hours)
- Capturing ideas in 5 different apps (you'll never find them)
- Only capturing when you sit down to create (too late)
- Scoring every idea (only score when you have 10+ and need to choose)
