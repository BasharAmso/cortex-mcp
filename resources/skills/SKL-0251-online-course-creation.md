## Core Concepts

Tutor's architecture for Open edX reveals a principle that applies to all course creation: modularity wins. Just as Tutor uses a plugin-based system where features are added without modifying the core, your course should be built as self-contained modules that can be rearranged, updated, or expanded independently.

### Course Structure Framework

Organize content in three layers:

1. **Module** (big topic, 3-7 per course) — Each module is a standalone learning unit with a clear outcome. A student who completes only one module should still gain something useful.
2. **Lesson** (single concept, 3-5 per module) — One idea per lesson. If you need more than 15 minutes to teach it, split it.
3. **Activity** (practice element, 1-2 per lesson) — Quizzes, exercises, worksheets, or reflection prompts. Learning without doing produces zero retention.

### Platform Selection Matrix

| Factor | Self-Hosted (Tutor/Open edX) | Marketplace (Udemy, Skillshare) | Creator Platform (Teachable, Podia) |
|--------|------------------------------|--------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| **Control** | Full | Low | Medium |
| **Audience** | You bring your own | Built-in discovery | You bring your own |
| **Pricing** | You set it | Platform controls | You set it |
| **Revenue** | 100% (minus hosting) | 37-97% to platform | 5-10% platform fee |
| **Best for** | Organizations, branded academies | Audience discovery | Independent creators |

For most personal-brand builders, start with a creator platform. Self-hosting only makes sense once you have a proven curriculum and 500+ students.

### Pricing Strategy

Price based on the outcome, not the hours of content. A 2-hour course that teaches someone to land freelance clients is worth more than a 40-hour course covering theory. Three tiers work well:

- **Core** ($97-197) — Video lessons and materials only
- **Premium** ($297-497) — Core + community access + templates
- **VIP** ($997+) — Premium + live coaching calls or 1-on-1 feedback

### Launch Sequence

1. **Validate** — Sell 10 pre-orders before recording a single lesson. If you cannot sell 10, the topic or positioning needs work.
2. **Pilot** — Deliver the first cohort live (Zoom or group calls). Record everything. Student questions reveal what you missed.
3. **Package** — Edit recordings, add worksheets, build the on-demand version from proven content.
4. **Launch** — Email list first, social media second, paid ads only after organic converts.

## Key Takeaways

- Structure courses as independent modules, not a single linear path
- Choose your platform based on audience ownership and revenue control, not features
- Price on transformation value, not content length
- Validate demand with pre-sales before investing in production
- Launch live first, then package the proven material into self-paced format
