## Core Concepts

Inbound sales converts interest into pipeline by meeting prospects where they already are: your website, app, or support channels. The key shift from outbound is that the buyer has already raised their hand. Your job is speed and relevance, not persuasion.

### The Speed-to-Lead Principle

Research consistently shows that responding to an inbound lead within 5 minutes yields dramatically higher conversion rates than responding within 30 minutes. Chatwoot and similar omnichannel platforms solve this by routing conversations to available agents in real time, with chatbot pre-qualification as a fallback when no human is online.

### Qualification Before Routing

Not every inbound message is a sales opportunity. A practical qualification flow asks three things before routing to a sales rep:

1. **Intent signal** - Are they asking about pricing, features, or a specific use case? Or just browsing documentation?
2. **Fit signal** - Do they match your ideal customer profile (company size, industry, role)?
3. **Urgency signal** - Are they evaluating now, or researching for the future?

Chatbots handle this with structured questions or keyword detection. The goal is to route high-intent, high-fit leads to sales immediately and nurture the rest via marketing automation.

### Channel Unification

Modern inbound sales spans live chat widgets, email, social DMs, WhatsApp, and SMS. Chatwoot's architecture treats all of these as "conversations" routed to a single inbox. The sales benefit: reps see full conversation history regardless of which channel the prospect used, eliminating the "can you repeat your question" friction that kills deals.

### Handoff Design

The transition from bot to human and from marketing to sales must be seamless. Best practices:
- **Warm handoff**: the bot summarizes what it learned before connecting to a rep ("This visitor is from a 50-person SaaS company, asking about enterprise pricing").
- **Canned responses**: pre-written replies for common early-stage questions so reps respond fast without sounding robotic.
- **Availability routing**: if no sales rep is online, capture the lead's email and schedule a callback rather than letting the conversation die.

### Measuring Inbound Effectiveness

Track four metrics: **response time** (first reply), **qualification rate** (percentage of conversations that become opportunities), **chat-to-meeting rate**, and **channel attribution** (which channels produce the highest-value leads).

## Key Takeaways

- Speed is the single biggest lever in inbound sales; automate first response to buy time for human follow-up.
- Qualify before routing so sales reps spend time on high-fit, high-intent leads only.
- Unify all inbound channels into one inbox to prevent leads from falling through cracks.
- Design bot-to-human handoffs that carry context forward so prospects never repeat themselves.
- Measure response time and chat-to-meeting rate as your primary inbound health indicators.
