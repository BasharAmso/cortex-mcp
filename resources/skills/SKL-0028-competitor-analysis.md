# Competitor Analysis

Understand the competitive landscape before committing to a product direction. Applies professional PM frameworks for competitive intelligence, positioning, and strategic differentiation. Outputs feed into PRD Writing, Problem Stress Test, and Pitch Deck.

## Competitor Categories

| Type | Definition | Why It Matters |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Direct | Same problem, same audience, same approach | Your primary competition for users |
| Indirect | Same problem, different approach (manual processes, spreadsheets, workarounds) | Often the real competition; inertia is the hardest competitor |
| Adjacent | Different problem, overlapping audience | May expand into your space |
| Aspirational | Products whose quality or UX you want to match | Design benchmarks, not competitors |

Aim for 3-5 direct + 2-3 indirect. If you cannot find direct competitors, either the market does not exist or you are not looking hard enough.

## Procedure

### 1. Identify Competitors

Search Product Hunt, G2, Capterra, App Store, and relevant subreddits. Ask: "What do people use today to solve this problem?" Include manual workarounds as indirect competitors.

### 2. Research Each Competitor

| Dimension | What to Find | Where to Look |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Product | Core features, unique capabilities | Product website, demos, free trials |
| Pricing | Free tier, price points, model (per-seat, flat, usage) | Pricing page, review sites |
| Positioning | How they describe themselves | Homepage hero text, tagline |
| Audience | Who their customers are | Testimonials, case studies, job postings |
| Strengths | What users praise | 5-star reviews, social media |
| Weaknesses | What users complain about | 1-2 star reviews, forum complaints, churn reasons |
| Traction | Market presence signals | App store ratings, employee count, funding |

### 3. Build Comparison Matrix

Create a table comparing your product against each competitor across: core value prop, key differentiator, price point, target audience, biggest strength, biggest weakness.

### 4. Identify Gaps and Opportunities

Systematically look for:

- **Underserved segments:** User groups no competitor serves well
- **Feature gaps:** Capabilities nobody offers that users request
- **Price gaps:** Overpriced or underpriced tiers
- **UX gaps:** Workflow friction that competitors accept as normal
- **Positioning gaps:** Unclaimed narrative angles

### 5. Define Differentiation Strategy

| Element | Description |
|---------|------------|
| Primary differentiator | The one defensible reason to choose your product |
| Supporting differentiators | 2-3 secondary advantages |
| Parity features | Table stakes you must match to be considered |
| Deliberate omissions | What competitors do that you intentionally will not |

## Output

Save to `docs/COMPETITOR_ANALYSIS.md`. Log key findings in research notes.

## Key Rules

- Never fabricate competitor data. Mark unverifiable information as "unconfirmed."
- Never disparage competitors. Focus on factual observations.
- Differentiation must be based on real gaps, not wishful thinking.
- "No competitors" is almost always wrong. Dig deeper.
