## Core Concepts

Content pillars are the 3-5 recurring topics that define what you are known for. Ghost's most successful publications have a clear editorial identity because they resist the temptation to cover everything. The same principle applies to personal brands: specificity creates recognition, and recognition creates authority.

### Defining Your Pillars

Choose 3-5 pillars at the intersection of three criteria:

1. **Expertise** — You have real experience and can share original insights (not just summaries of others' work)
2. **Audience demand** — People actively search for, ask about, or discuss this topic
3. **Energy** — You can write about this topic repeatedly without burning out

Test each candidate pillar: "Can I write 20 different posts on this topic?" If yes, it qualifies. If you struggle to imagine 10, it is a topic, not a pillar.

### Pillar Architecture

Each pillar should support multiple content types and depths:

| Layer | Description | Example (for "AI in Product Management" pillar) |
|-------|-------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| **Educational** | Teach a concept or framework | "How to evaluate whether your product needs AI" |
| **Experience** | Share a personal story or lesson | "What I learned shipping my first AI feature" |
| **Opinion** | Take a position on a debate | "Why most AI product roadmaps are backwards" |
| **Tactical** | Step-by-step how-to | "3-step process for writing AI feature specs" |
| **Curated** | Aggregate and comment on others' work | "5 AI product launches worth studying this month" |

This ensures variety within each pillar so your content never feels repetitive even though your topics are consistent.

### Rotation Cadence

Rotate across pillars to maintain balance. Two proven approaches:

**Weekly rotation** — Assign each day a pillar:
| Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Pillar 1 | Pillar 2 | Pillar 3 | Pillar 4 | Wildcard |

**Cycle rotation** — Post from each pillar in sequence regardless of day. Track position in a simple counter.

The wildcard slot handles timely topics, collaborations, or experiments. Keep it to 20% or less of your total output.

### Content Ratio Rule

For each pillar, maintain an approximate ratio:

- **70% value content** — Educational, tactical, curated (gives the audience what they came for)
- **20% story content** — Experience posts, behind-the-scenes (builds connection)
- **10% promotional** — Your product, service, newsletter, or event (earns revenue)

Invert this ratio and you become a billboard. Audiences unfollow billboards.

### Pillar Audit (Monthly)

Once a month, review your last 30 days of content:

| Check | Action if Failing |
|-------|-------------------|
| Are all pillars represented? | Prioritize the neglected pillar next week |
| Is one pillar dominating (>50%)? | Intentionally create content for others |
| Is the value/story/promo ratio held? | Reduce promotional content |
| Are posts within pillars varied by type? | Add a content type you have not used recently |

### Example Pillar Set

For a senior product manager building a personal brand:

1. **AI Product Strategy** — How to evaluate, spec, and ship AI features
2. **Enterprise PM Life** — Navigating large-org politics, stakeholders, and process
3. **Career Growth** — Lessons from 20 years in tech, advice for PMs at all levels
4. **Building in Public** — Behind-the-scenes of side projects and tools

## Key Takeaways

- Limit yourself to 3-5 pillars at the intersection of expertise, demand, and energy
- Each pillar supports multiple content types: educational, experience, opinion, tactical, curated
- Rotate across pillars weekly or cyclically to maintain balance
- Maintain a 70/20/10 ratio of value, story, and promotional content
- Audit monthly to catch drift before it becomes a pattern
