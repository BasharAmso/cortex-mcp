# YouTube Scripting

Script videos that hold attention. The indie maker community has demonstrated that YouTube content drives long-term, compounding traffic. Unlike social posts that decay in hours, a well-optimized video generates views for years. Bootstrapped founders use YouTube for tutorials, build-in-public vlogs, and educational content that doubles as marketing.

## Design the Thumbnail FIRST

The thumbnail is the most important part of your video. Script around it:

1. **Before scripting**, ask: "What single image would make someone click?"
2. Design or sketch the thumbnail concept before writing a word
3. The thumbnail and title must tell a complete story together (no redundancy)
4. Use the thumbnail as your creative constraint. The video must deliver what it promises

**Thumbnail principles:**
- One clear focal point (face, object, or result)
- 3 words maximum on the thumbnail. Readable at mobile size
- High contrast colors. Avoid blue (blends with YouTube UI)
- Show emotion on faces. Exaggerated expressions outperform neutral
- Before/after or contrast images drive curiosity

## Hook-Content-CTA Structure

### Hook (0-30 seconds)

The hook determines whether 70% of viewers stay or leave:

- **Pattern interrupt** -- Start mid-action, mid-sentence, or with a surprising visual
- **Promise** -- State exactly what the viewer will get ("By the end of this video, you'll know...")
- **Proof** -- Show the end result immediately (then explain how)
- **Problem** -- Name the pain they're feeling right now

Never start with "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel."

### Content (middle 70-80%)

Structure the body using one of these frameworks:

| Framework | Best For | Structure |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Listicle | Tips, tools, mistakes | Numbered items with mini-hooks between |
| Story | Personal experience, case studies | Setup, conflict, resolution, lesson |
| Tutorial | How-to content | Step-by-step with clear visual markers |
| Myth-busting | Contrarian takes | Common belief, why it's wrong, what to do instead |

**Retention tactics within the body:**
- Re-hook every 2-3 minutes ("But here's where it gets interesting...")
- Use pattern interrupts: B-roll changes, graphics, camera angle shifts
- Open loops: tease upcoming points ("I'll show you the best one at #3")
- Remove dead air. Every second must earn its place

### CTA (final 30-60 seconds)

- Deliver on the hook's promise first. Then ask
- One primary CTA only (subscribe, watch next video, or visit link)
- Use an end screen pointing to a related video

## YouTube for Indie Makers

Successful bootstrapped creators use video as a top-of-funnel marketing channel:

| Content Strategy | Example | Business Impact |
|-----------------|---------|-----------------|
| Tutorial solving your product's use case | "How to automate X" (using your tool) | Organic leads who already understand the value |
| Build-in-public vlog | Monthly revenue updates, feature launches | Audience investment in your success |
| Niche education | Deep dives on your area of expertise | Authority that transfers to product trust |
| Tool comparisons | Honest reviews including your product | Captures buyers actively evaluating options |

## YouTube Shorts Strategy

Shorts feed a different audience but funnel subscribers to long-form:

- 30-59 seconds is the sweet spot
- Hook in the first 2 seconds (no intro, no logo)
- Vertical format, text overlays for silent viewing
- End with a reason to watch again (loop-friendly content performs best)
- Post 3-5 Shorts per week to test topics before committing to long-form

## SEO for YouTube

- **Title:** Front-load the primary keyword. Keep under 60 characters
- **Description:** First 2 lines appear in search. Include keyword naturally, then detailed summary
- **Tags:** 5-8 tags mixing broad and specific terms
- **Chapters:** Add timestamps in description. YouTube shows these in search results
- **Closed captions:** Upload corrected auto-captions. YouTube indexes the text

## Key Rules

- Never upload without watching the full video at 1x speed first
- Script to a specific viewer, not "everyone"
- If you would skip it while watching, cut it from the script
- Thumbnail and title are 80% of a video's success. Spend time there
