## Core Concepts

Ghost's content strategy principle applies directly to YouTube: own your audience through consistent, valuable publishing on a platform you understand deeply. Ghost builds newsletters alongside blog posts to maintain direct audience relationships. On YouTube, the parallel is building an email list alongside your channel so the algorithm never fully controls your reach.

### Content Pillar System

Define 3-5 content pillars (recurring topics) and rotate between them. This builds topical authority while keeping your channel from becoming stale.

**Example for a tech personal brand:**
1. Tutorials (how-to walkthroughs)
2. Industry analysis (opinion/commentary)
3. Behind the scenes (process, failures, lessons)
4. Tool reviews (practical comparisons)

Every video should fit one pillar. If it doesn't fit any, it doesn't belong on your channel.

### Title + Thumbnail Framework

Titles and thumbnails are a single unit. Design them together, not separately.

**Title rules:**
- Front-load the value proposition: "Build a SaaS in 48 Hours" beats "My Weekend Project Building a SaaS App"
- Include a number or timeframe when possible
- 50-60 characters maximum for full visibility on mobile
- Avoid clickbait that your content cannot deliver on. Broken promises kill retention.

**Thumbnail rules:**
- Maximum 3 elements: face (with emotion), text (3-5 words), context image
- High contrast colors. Test at mobile size (120x90px) since that is where most impressions happen
- The thumbnail text should complement the title, not repeat it
- Faces with genuine emotion outperform text-only thumbnails by 2-3x on CTR

### YouTube SEO Basics

1. **Keyword in title** — Use YouTube search autocomplete to find what people actually search for
2. **First 2 sentences of description** — Include your target keyword naturally. YouTube reads this for ranking.
3. **Tags** — 5-8 relevant tags. Include your channel name as a tag for brand association.
4. **Chapters** — Add timestamps. YouTube uses these for search features and they improve watch time by letting viewers jump to what they need.
5. **Cards and end screens** — Link to related videos. Every video should point to another video.

### Consistency Over Quality

Publishing consistently matters more than production quality, especially in the first 50 videos. A weekly video shot on a phone with good audio beats a monthly cinematic production.

**Minimum viable setup:**
- Smartphone or webcam with decent lighting (a $20 ring light works)
- External microphone ($50-100 USB mic). Audio quality is the one thing viewers will not forgive.
- Free editing software (DaVinci Resolve, CapCut)
- A quiet room

### Monetization Path

1. **0-1K subscribers** — Focus on content and consistency. No monetization.
2. **1K-10K** — YouTube Partner Program (1K subs + 4K watch hours). Ad revenue is small but symbolic.
3. **10K+** — Sponsorships become viable. Promote your own products (courses, coaching, newsletter).
4. **50K+** — Brand deals, premium sponsorships, community membership.

The real monetization is never ad revenue. It is using YouTube as the top of your funnel for courses, coaching, and consulting.

## Key Takeaways

- Define 3-5 content pillars and only publish videos that fit one of them
- Design titles and thumbnails as a single unit, testing at mobile size
- Prioritize consistent weekly publishing over high production quality
- Use YouTube SEO basics: keyword in title, description, chapters, and tags
- Treat YouTube as a funnel to your own products, not as a primary revenue source
