## Core Concepts

Twitter/X rewards speed, specificity, and conversation. Ghost's publishing model emphasizes owning your audience through your own platform, but Twitter/X is the best discovery engine for driving people to that owned platform. The strategy: use X for reach, move your best audience to email or a blog.

### Content Type Mix

Vary your content across these types. Each serves a different purpose:

| Content Type | Purpose | Frequency | Example |
|-------------|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Atomic insight** | Demonstrate expertise in one tweet | Daily | "The biggest mistake in API design: making everything RESTful when some operations are clearly commands" |
| **Thread** | Deep-dive that builds authority | 1-2x/week | 7-tweet breakdown of a framework or lesson |
| **Reply** | Join conversations, get discovered | 5-10x/day | Add value to posts from larger accounts in your niche |
| **Hot take** | Spark discussion, test ideas | 2-3x/week | Contrarian opinion on a trending topic |
| **Retweet + commentary** | Show taste and perspective | Daily | Quote tweet with your specific angle |

### Thread Writing Formula

Threads are the highest-leverage content type on X. Structure matters:

1. **Hook tweet** — Must stand alone as a compelling statement. This is the only tweet most people see. No "Thread:" label needed.
2. **Promise** — Tweet 2 tells the reader what they will learn and why it matters.
3. **Body** (3-7 tweets) — One idea per tweet. Use line breaks for readability. Concrete examples beat abstract advice.
4. **Summary** — Restate the key points in a quick list.
5. **CTA** — Ask for a follow, a retweet, or link to your newsletter. Only one CTA, not three.

### Engagement Strategy

Growth on X is driven by outbound engagement more than posting:

- **Comment on 10-15 posts daily** from accounts in your niche. Add genuine value, not "Great post!" comments.
- **Reply within the first hour** of posts from larger accounts. Early replies get more visibility.
- **Ask questions** that invite others to share their experience. Questions consistently outperform statements for engagement rate.
- **Acknowledge and amplify** others' good work. Generosity is the fastest relationship-building tool on the platform.

### Daily Routine (30 minutes)

| Time Block | Activity | Duration |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Morning | Engage: reply to 10 posts in your niche | 10 min |
| Midday | Post: publish your prepared content | 5 min |
| Afternoon | Engage: respond to replies on your posts | 10 min |
| Evening | Capture: save ideas for tomorrow's content | 5 min |

### Growth Tactics

- **Build in public** — Share your work, wins, failures, and numbers. Transparency generates trust and engagement.
- **Create recurring formats** — "Every Monday I share one thing I learned last week" gives followers a reason to return.
- **Cross-pollinate** — Turn your best tweets into LinkedIn posts, newsletter sections, or blog intros. Content should flow between platforms.
- **Pin your best tweet** — Your pinned tweet is your billboard. Update it to your most relevant, high-performing post.

### What to Avoid

- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree") without substance
- Posting threads with no line breaks (unreadable)
- Only tweeting and never engaging with others
- Copying trending formats without adding your perspective
- Going viral on an off-topic tweet that attracts the wrong audience

## Key Takeaways

- Mix content types: atomic insights for daily presence, threads for authority, replies for discovery
- Thread hooks must stand alone; if the first tweet is weak, nobody reads tweet two
- Outbound engagement (commenting on others' posts) drives growth faster than posting alone
- Keep a consistent 30-minute daily routine rather than sporadic marathon sessions
- Use X for discovery, then funnel your best audience to owned platforms like email
