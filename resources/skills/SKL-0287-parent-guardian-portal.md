## Core Concepts

Parents and guardians need visibility into student progress without the ability to modify learning content or submit work. The portal is a read-heavy, notification-driven interface with strict permission boundaries.

### Account Linking Model

A guardian account links to one or more student accounts. The data model needs three entities:

- **Guardian** — their own auth credentials, profile, notification preferences
- **Student** — the learner account with course enrollments and submissions
- **GuardianLink** — join table with `guardian_id`, `student_id`, `relationship` (parent, guardian, tutor), `status` (pending, active, revoked), and `linked_at` timestamp

Linking flow: Guardian enters a student invite code (generated by the institution or student). The student or admin approves the link. Never allow self-linking without verification — this prevents unauthorized access to minors' data.

### Permission Boundaries

Guardians see a curated subset of student data:

| Can View | Cannot View | Cannot Do |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Course enrollment list | Private journal entries | Submit assignments |
| Grade summaries and trends | Peer discussion posts | Modify course content |
| Assignment due dates | Direct messages to peers | Change student settings |
| Attendance records | Assessment answer details | Access other students |
| Teacher announcements | Draft/incomplete work | Impersonate student |

Implement this as a `guardian` role in your RBAC system with explicit allow-list scopes, not by inheriting the student role and removing permissions.

### Progress Dashboard

The guardian dashboard prioritizes at-a-glance status:

1. **Per-course summary cards** — course name, current grade (letter + percentage), assignments completed vs total, next due date. Color-code status: on track (green), falling behind (yellow), at risk (red) — always with text labels alongside color for accessibility.

2. **Weekly digest** — automated email or push notification summarizing: grades changed, assignments submitted, assignments overdue, upcoming deadlines. Configurable frequency (daily, weekly, off).

3. **Grade trend chart** — simple line chart showing grade trajectory over the term. Parents care about direction more than individual scores.

### Communication Channels

Parent-teacher messaging should be scoped and structured:
- Messages are tied to a specific student and optionally a specific course
- Teachers can broadcast to all guardians of a class
- All messages are logged and visible to institution admins (compliance)
- No direct student-to-guardian messaging through the platform (use external channels)

### Privacy and Compliance

- **COPPA** (under 13) and **FERPA** (US education records) govern what data can be shared and with whom
- Guardian access must be revocable by the institution at any time
- Audit log every guardian data access event
- Age-gated features: if the student is 18+, guardian linking requires student consent

## Key Takeaways

- Guardian accounts are read-only views with explicit allow-list permissions, never inherited student roles
- Account linking requires verification (invite codes + approval) to protect student privacy
- Weekly digest notifications are the highest-value feature — most guardians check passively
- FERPA and COPPA compliance shapes the entire permission model for US-based platforms
- Always pair color-coded status indicators with text labels for accessibility
