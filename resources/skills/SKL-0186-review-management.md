## Core Concepts

Review management is the practice of monitoring, responding to, and generating customer reviews to build business reputation. Chatwoot's open-source customer communication platform demonstrates how to centralize feedback from multiple channels into one actionable system.

### Unified Inbox for Reviews

The biggest review management mistake is checking each platform separately. Chatwoot solves this by pulling conversations from website chat, email, Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, WhatsApp, Telegram, and SMS into a single inbox. Apply the same principle to reviews: aggregate Google Business, Yelp, Facebook Reviews, and industry-specific platforms into one dashboard. Each incoming review becomes a conversation that needs a response.

### Response Strategy

Every review deserves a response. For **positive reviews**: thank the customer by name, reference something specific about their experience, and invite them back. For **negative reviews**: acknowledge the issue, apologize without excuses, offer a concrete resolution (refund, redo, discount), and move the conversation to a private channel (email or DM) for details. Chatwoot's canned responses feature shows the value of having pre-approved response templates that staff can personalize, not copy-paste verbatim.

### Response Timing

Speed matters. Aim to respond to all reviews within 24 hours, negative reviews within 4 hours. Chatwoot's auto-assignment and routing features demonstrate how to ensure no review sits unaddressed. Set up notifications so the right team member gets alerted immediately. Use labels to categorize reviews by sentiment (positive, neutral, negative) and topic (service, product, pricing).

### Review Generation

Happy customers rarely leave reviews unprompted. Build review requests into your post-service workflow: send an SMS or email 2 hours after service completion with a direct link to your Google Business review page. Chatwoot's automation capabilities can trigger these follow-ups automatically. The key insight from CSAT reporting: identify customers who gave high satisfaction scores and specifically ask them for public reviews.

### Measuring Reputation

Track these metrics monthly: **review volume** (are you getting enough reviews?), **average rating** (trend over time), **response rate** (percentage of reviews you replied to), and **response time** (how fast you reply). Chatwoot provides agent performance metrics and downloadable reports that map to this kind of tracking. Set a target: 4.5+ stars with 90%+ response rate.

### AI-Assisted Management

Chatwoot's AI agent ("Captain") shows the direction: AI can draft initial review responses, categorize sentiment automatically, and flag urgent negative reviews. Use AI as a first draft, but always have a human review before posting. Automated responses that feel robotic damage trust more than slow responses.

## Key Takeaways

- Centralize all review sources into a single inbox so nothing gets missed
- Respond to every review: positive within 24 hours, negative within 4 hours
- Use templates as starting points but personalize every response
- Build automated review requests into your post-service workflow targeting satisfied customers
- Track review volume, average rating, response rate, and response time monthly
