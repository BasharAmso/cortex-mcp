# Copywriting Formulas

Formulas give you structure so you can focus on the message. Pick the right one for your situation, fill in the blanks, and refine. The best copy follows formula for structure but breaks rules at the sentence level: active voice, no filler, every word earning its place.

## The Five Core Formulas

### 1. AIDA (Attention - Interest - Desire - Action)

The classic. Works almost anywhere.

- **Attention:** Hook with a bold claim or surprising fact
- **Interest:** Explain why this matters to them specifically
- **Desire:** Show the outcome they want, with proof
- **Action:** Tell them exactly what to do next

*Best for: Landing pages, long-form ads, product launches.*

### 2. PAS (Problem - Agitate - Solve)

Works when your audience already feels the pain.

- **Problem:** Name the struggle they're having right now
- **Agitate:** Make the cost of inaction vivid and specific
- **Solve:** Present your solution as the natural relief

*Best for: Email subject lines, social posts, retargeting ads.*

### 3. BAB (Before - After - Bridge)

Works when you're selling a transformation.

- **Before:** Describe their current frustrating reality
- **After:** Paint the picture of life after the solution
- **Bridge:** Reveal how to get from before to after

*Best for: Testimonials, case studies, about pages, course sales.*

### 4. 4Ps (Promise - Picture - Proof - Push)

Works when you need to build trust quickly.

- **Promise:** Make a specific, believable claim
- **Picture:** Help them visualize having the result
- **Proof:** Back it up with evidence (numbers, testimonials, demos)
- **Push:** Create urgency or a clear next step

*Best for: Feature sections, pricing pages, webinar signups.*

### 5. FAB (Feature - Advantage - Benefit)

Works for turning specs into reasons to buy.

- **Feature:** What it is or does (technical fact)
- **Advantage:** Why that feature matters (compared to alternatives)
- **Benefit:** What it means for the reader's life (emotional payoff)

*Best for: Product descriptions, feature comparison pages, spec sheets.*

## Prose Quality in Copy

Automated prose analysis reveals patterns that weaken conversion copy. Apply these checks to every formula output:

| Bad Pattern | Why It Kills Conversion | Fix |
|------------|------------------------|-----|
| Passive voice | Hides who benefits and who acts | "Your time is saved" becomes "Save 3 hours every week" |
| Weasel words ("some," "many," "often") | Undermines proof and specificity | Replace with real numbers or cut entirely |
| Adverb padding ("very unique," "really fast") | Signals weak claims | Use a stronger noun or verb instead |
| Wordy phrases ("in order to," "due to the fact that") | Slows the reader down | Compress: "to," "because" |
| Cliches ("game-changer," "best-in-class") | Reader's brain skips them | Replace with a specific, original claim |

## Decision Table

| Situation | Recommended Formula | Why |
|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| Cold audience, first impression | AIDA | Needs full attention-building sequence |
| Audience knows the problem exists | PAS | Skip awareness, go straight to pain |
| Selling a transformation or result | BAB | Contrast drives desire |
| Need to build credibility fast | 4Ps | Proof is front and center |
| Describing product features | FAB | Converts specs into benefits |
| Short social post (under 100 words) | PAS or BAB | Compact enough for short-form |
| Email sequence (multi-touch) | AIDA across sequence | Each email maps to one letter |
| Testimonial or case study | BAB | Natural story arc |

## Applying Formulas by Content Type

### Landing Page Structure (AIDA)

1. Hero section = Attention (headline + subhead)
2. Problem/benefit section = Interest
3. Social proof + outcomes = Desire
4. CTA section = Action

### Sales Email (PAS)

- Subject line = Problem (name it in 5 words)
- First paragraph = Agitate (what it costs them)
- Body = Solve (your offer as the answer)
- PS line = Second CTA or urgency

### Social Post (BAB)

- Line 1-2 = Before (relatable struggle)
- Line 3-4 = After (vivid result)
- Line 5-6 = Bridge (the method or insight)
- Last line = CTA or question

## Feature-to-Benefit Conversion

Practice this translation for everything you write:

| Feature (What) | Advantage (So What) | Benefit (Why Care) |
|----------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| AI-powered search | Finds results in 2 seconds | Stop wasting 10 minutes digging through files |
| Mobile app | Access anywhere | Check progress from your kid's soccer game |
| One-click export | No manual formatting | Spend your Friday afternoon not fighting spreadsheets |

## Key Rules

- One formula per piece. Don't mix AIDA and PAS in the same section.
- Always lead with the reader's problem, not your product's features.
- Every piece of copy needs exactly one clear CTA. Not two. Not zero.
- Read it aloud. If you stumble on a phrase, rewrite it. Wordy or passive constructions are the usual culprit.
