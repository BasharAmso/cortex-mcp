## Core Concepts

Cal.com's scheduling architecture models the core truth of coaching: the meeting is the product. Just as Cal.com gives full control over availability, booking flows, and calendar ownership, your coaching practice needs clear boundaries, structured sessions, and systems that protect your time while making it easy for clients to book.

### Session Structure Framework

Every coaching session follows the GROW model adapted for practical mentoring:

1. **Goal** (5 min) — "What do you want to walk away with today?" Pin down one specific outcome. Vague goals produce vague sessions.
2. **Reality** (10 min) — "Where are you right now on this?" Map the current state. Ask, don't assume. The mentee's perception of the gap matters more than yours.
3. **Options** (15 min) — "What have you tried? What else could work?" Generate 3-5 possibilities together. Your job is to expand their options, not prescribe answers.
4. **Way Forward** (10 min) — "What will you do before our next session?" One concrete action. Write it down. Follow up on it next time.

### Scheduling and Tooling

Set up scheduling that respects both parties:

- **Availability windows** — Block specific days/times for coaching. Cal.com's model of letting you define granular availability prevents calendar chaos.
- **Buffer time** — Add 15 minutes between sessions for notes and mental reset. Back-to-back calls lead to burnout and sloppy advice.
- **Intake form** — Require a brief pre-session questionnaire. Three questions: what is your biggest challenge, what have you tried, what would success look like?
- **Calendar ownership** — Use tools where you own the data (Cal.com, Calendly). Avoid platform lock-in where your client relationships live on someone else's server.

### Pricing Models

| Model | Structure | Best For |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Per session** | $100-300/hour | Starting out, testing demand |
| **Package** | 4 sessions for 3x single price | Committed clients, better outcomes |
| **Monthly retainer** | $500-2000/month for async + calls | Ongoing relationships, premium clients |
| **Group coaching** | $50-150/person, 5-10 per group | Scaling beyond 1-on-1 capacity |

Start with per-session pricing. Move to packages once you have repeat clients. Group coaching is where leverage lives, but only after you have refined your framework through dozens of 1-on-1 conversations.

### Building Your Coaching Framework

The best coaches are not the smartest people in the room. They are the most structured. Build a repeatable framework:

1. **Name it** — Give your method a name. "The 90-Day Career Sprint" is more compelling than "coaching calls."
2. **Document it** — Write down your 5-7 step process. This becomes your sales page and your session guide.
3. **Template it** — Create session templates, action item trackers, and progress scorecards.

## Key Takeaways

- Structure every session with a clear framework (GROW or similar) to avoid aimless conversations
- Set up scheduling with buffer time and intake forms before accepting your first client
- Start with per-session pricing, move to packages once demand is proven
- Build and name a repeatable coaching framework to differentiate from generic mentors
- Own your scheduling data and client relationships on platforms you control
