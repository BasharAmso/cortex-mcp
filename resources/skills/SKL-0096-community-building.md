# Community Building

Turn a passive audience into an active community. The indie maker ecosystem thrives on community: IndieHackers, MakerLog, MegaMaker Club, FounderCafe, Nugget.one, and the Bootstrapped Slack each serve a specific niche of builders. A community is not a group chat. It is a space where members connect with each other, not just with you.

## Platform Selection

| Platform | Best For | Tradeoff |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Discord | Real-time interaction, younger/tech audiences | High noise, requires active moderation |
| Slack | Professional communities, async discussion | Feels like work, free tier limits history |
| Circle | Paid communities, course cohorts | Monthly cost, less spontaneous interaction |
| Skool | Creator-led communities with courses | Gamification built in, platform lock-in |
| GitHub Discussions | Open source, developer communities | Limited to technical audiences |

Pick one. Do not split your community across platforms.

## Lessons from Indie Communities

Successful indie communities share common patterns:

| Community | Model | Key Insight |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| IndieHackers | Free, interview-driven | Content attracts members; community retains them |
| MakerLog | Free, task-based streaks | Gamification and accountability keep people coming back |
| MegaMaker Club | Paid, curated | Exclusivity + curation creates higher-quality conversation |
| FounderCafe | Paid, invite-only | Small, high-trust groups produce better help than large ones |
| Bootstrapped Slack | Free, organic | Low moderation works when the niche self-selects quality members |

## Community Setup Essentials

1. **Welcome channel:** Auto-greet new members with clear instructions on what to do first
2. **Introduction prompt:** Give a template ("Share your name, what you do, and one thing you are working on")
3. **3-5 focused channels:** Start minimal. Add channels only when organic conversation demands it
4. **Rules and expectations:** Short, clear, positive-framed ("Do share works-in-progress" not just "Don't spam")
5. **Pinned resources:** Best content, FAQs, community wins

## Engagement Loops

Members stay when they get value on a predictable rhythm:

| Frequency | Activity | Purpose |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Daily | Question of the day, share-your-work thread | Low-effort participation habit |
| Weekly | Live session, challenge, or themed discussion | Anchor event members look forward to |
| Monthly | Member spotlight, retrospective, or AMA | Recognition and reflection |
| Quarterly | Community project, collab, or milestone celebration | Shared accomplishment |

The first 30 days matter most. New members who do not engage in week one rarely come back.

## Member Journey

Design a path from stranger to champion:

1. **Lurker:** Reads but does not post. Lower the barrier with reaction-only prompts.
2. **Participant:** Responds to prompts and discussions. Acknowledge them by name.
3. **Contributor:** Starts conversations, helps others. Give them a role or title.
4. **Champion:** Actively recruits, moderates, creates content. Involve them in decisions.

## Converting Audience to Community

- Mention the community in 1 out of every 5-10 posts (not every post)
- Gate with a simple application or question to filter for quality
- Offer something exclusive: early access, behind-the-scenes, direct access to you
- Launch with a founding member cohort (50-100 people) before opening broadly

## Moderation Principles

- Set tone by example in the first 100 messages
- Address bad behavior privately first, publicly only if it continues
- Appoint trusted members as moderators early (you cannot scale alone)
- Remove toxic members quickly. One bad actor drives away ten good members

## Community-Led Growth

A healthy community grows itself when:
- Members share wins publicly and tag the community
- You turn community discussions into public content (with permission)
- Members invite peers because they genuinely get value
- The community creates artifacts (guides, templates, projects) that attract outsiders
