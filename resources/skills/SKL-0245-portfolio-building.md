## Core Concepts

A portfolio is proof-of-work for your professional brand. It answers the question every potential client, employer, or collaborator has: "Can this person actually deliver?" The best portfolios show outcomes, not just outputs.

### Curate Ruthlessly

Quality beats quantity every time. Feature three to five of your strongest projects rather than listing everything you have ever done. Each project should demonstrate a different skill or solve a different type of problem. Ghost, the open-source publishing platform, demonstrates this principle in their own showcase: they highlight a curated set of publisher success stories rather than listing every site ever built on their platform.

### The Case Study Structure

Every portfolio piece should follow a narrative arc that mirrors how professionals evaluate work:

1. **Context** (2 sentences): What was the situation? Who was the client or audience?
2. **Problem** (2 sentences): What specific challenge needed solving?
3. **Approach** (3 to 4 sentences): What did you do and why? What trade-offs did you make?
4. **Outcome** (2 sentences): What measurable result did your work produce?
5. **Reflection** (1 sentence): What would you do differently next time?

This structure works whether you are a developer, designer, writer, marketer, or product manager. The key is quantifying outcomes wherever possible: revenue generated, time saved, users gained, performance improved.

### Before-and-After Examples

Nothing communicates impact like a direct comparison. Ghost's publishing workflow shows this well: their editor displays the raw writing experience alongside the polished published output. Apply the same technique to your portfolio. Show the messy starting state and the clean result. For developers, this might be architecture diagrams before and after refactoring. For designers, the original wireframe versus the shipped UI. For writers, the first draft versus the final copy.

### Living Portfolio Strategy

Your portfolio should evolve as you do. Set a quarterly reminder to review and rotate projects. Remove work that no longer represents your current skill level. Add recent projects that show growth. Ghost's content management approach (draft, review, publish, update) applies perfectly: treat each portfolio piece as living content that gets revised, not as a static archive.

### Platform Choice

Host your portfolio where your audience expects to find it. Developers often use GitHub profiles and personal sites. Designers use Behance or Dribbble alongside personal sites. Writers and content creators benefit from platforms like Ghost that offer clean reading experiences with built-in SEO, membership features, and custom domains.

## Key Takeaways

- Feature three to five strong projects rather than everything you have done. Curate for quality.
- Structure each piece as a case study: context, problem, approach, outcome, reflection.
- Use before-and-after comparisons to make your impact immediately visible.
- Treat your portfolio as living content. Review and rotate quarterly.
- Quantify outcomes wherever possible. Numbers are more persuasive than descriptions.
