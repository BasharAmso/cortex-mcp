# LinkedIn Strategy

Build a LinkedIn presence that attracts opportunities. This playbook covers profile optimization, post formats, algorithm mechanics, and engagement tactics that compound over time.

## Profile Optimization

Your profile is your landing page. Optimize these elements in order:

1. **Headline** -- Lead with what you do for others, not your job title. Formula: "I help [audience] [achieve outcome] through [method]"
2. **Banner image** -- Reinforce your headline visually. Include a tagline or CTA
3. **About section** -- First 3 lines are visible before "see more." Hook immediately. Write in first person, tell your story, end with a CTA
4. **Featured section** -- Pin your best-performing post, a lead magnet, or portfolio piece
5. **Experience** -- Write achievements, not job descriptions. Use metrics

## Post Formats That Work

| Format | Best For | Avg. Reach |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Text-only (short) | Hot takes, personal stories | High |
| Text-only (long) | Frameworks, lessons learned | Medium-High |
| Carousel (PDF) | Step-by-step guides, listicles | Very High |
| Single image + text | Behind-the-scenes, milestones | Medium |
| Video (native) | Tutorials, talking head | Medium |
| Polls | Audience research, engagement bait | High reach, low quality |
| Document posts | In-depth guides, checklists | High |

## Algorithm Signals

LinkedIn rewards these behaviors in priority order:

1. **Dwell time** -- Longer posts that people actually read beat short viral hooks
2. **Early engagement** -- First 90 minutes matter most. Comments outweigh reactions
3. **Meaningful comments** -- Comments over 10 words signal quality conversation
4. **Shares to DM** -- Private shares are a strong signal
5. **Profile visits after viewing** -- Indicates compelling content
6. **Consistency** -- 3-5 posts per week outperforms sporadic viral attempts

## Commenting Strategy

Commenting is the most underrated growth lever:

- Comment on 10-15 posts from accounts larger than yours daily
- Add genuine value: share experience, ask a smart question, offer a different angle
- First-hour comments on popular posts get maximum visibility
- Never comment "Great post!" or emoji-only reactions
- Your comment is content. Write it like a mini-post

## Content Calendar Framework

| Day | Content Type | Goal |
|-----|-------------|------|
| Mon | Industry insight or trend | Authority |
| Tue | Personal story or lesson | Relatability |
| Wed | Carousel or framework | Saves and shares |
| Thu | Engagement post (question or contrarian take) | Comments |
| Fri | Behind-the-scenes or wins | Trust |

Batch-write 5 posts on the weekend. Schedule with Buffer or native LinkedIn scheduling.

## Engagement Pods: Pros and Cons

**Pros:** Early engagement boost, community support, accountability.
**Cons:** Algorithm detects artificial patterns, attracts low-quality followers, creates dependency. LinkedIn has actively penalized pod-driven engagement since 2024.

**Verdict:** Small organic pods (under 10 people in your niche) can help. Large automated pods hurt more than they help.

## Analytics to Track Weekly

- Impressions per post (trending up?)
- Engagement rate (comments + shares / impressions)
- Profile views (leading indicator of opportunities)
- Connection request rate after posting
- Top-performing post format and topic

## Key Rules

- Never post and ghost. Stay in the comments for 30 minutes after publishing
- Write for one specific person, not "my network"
- Personal stories outperform generic advice every time
- Consistency beats virality. Show up before you blow up
