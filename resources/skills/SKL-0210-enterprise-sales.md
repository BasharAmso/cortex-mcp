## Core Concepts

Enterprise sales is a fundamentally different discipline from SMB or mid-market selling. Deals take months, involve committees, require procurement navigation, and demand a strategic approach to relationship building. The reward is larger contracts, longer retention, and significant expansion potential.

### Stakeholder Mapping

Enterprise deals are won or lost based on internal politics. Map every stakeholder using this framework:

| Role | Who | What They Care About |
|------|-----|---------------------|
| **Economic Buyer** | Signs the check (CFO, VP) | ROI, risk reduction, budget fit |
| **Technical Evaluator** | Assesses the product (engineering, IT) | Architecture, security, integration |
| **Champion** | Advocates internally for you | Career advancement, solving their pain |
| **Coach** | Gives you intel on internal dynamics | Maintaining their reputation |
| **Blocker** | Opposes the deal | Protecting status quo, competing priorities |

Twenty CRM's customizable objects and fields reflect this reality: enterprise CRM must track not just companies and deals, but individual stakeholders, their roles, and their sentiment across the buying cycle.

### Building Champions

Your champion is the person inside the prospect who sells when you are not in the room. They need three things from you: (1) a compelling story they can retell, (2) ammunition to overcome internal objections, and (3) confidence that choosing you will not damage their career. Arm them with ROI calculators, customer references in their industry, and executive summaries they can forward up the chain.

### The Enterprise Sales Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Discovery | Weeks 1-4 | Map stakeholders, understand pain, qualify budget |
| Technical Validation | Weeks 4-8 | Proof of concept, security review, integration assessment |
| Business Case | Weeks 8-12 | ROI documentation, executive presentation |
| Procurement | Weeks 12-20+ | Legal review, MSA negotiation, vendor onboarding |

Twenty's workflow automation with triggers and actions helps track these phases systematically. Consolidate emails, calendar events, and files per account so nothing falls through the cracks during months-long cycles.

### Navigating Procurement

Enterprise procurement exists to protect the company, not to block your deal. Work with it, not around it. Provide security questionnaires proactively, offer references early, and have your legal team ready for MSA redlines. The vendor who makes procurement's job easy moves faster than the vendor who fights every clause.

### Multi-Threading

Never let a deal depend on a single contact. Engage at least three stakeholders at different levels: an executive sponsor, a mid-level champion, and a technical evaluator. If any one person changes roles or leaves, the deal survives. Activity tracking across all contacts, as Twenty's platform enables, is essential to maintaining these parallel threads.

## Key Takeaways

- Map every stakeholder by role, motivation, and sentiment before proposing solutions
- Build champions by arming them to sell internally, not just by impressing them
- Expect 12-20+ week cycles and plan your phases accordingly
- Work with procurement proactively rather than treating it as an obstacle
- Multi-thread across 3+ contacts to insulate deals from personnel changes
