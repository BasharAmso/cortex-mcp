# Newsletter Writing

Build a newsletter that people actually open. The indie maker community considers email the most valuable owned channel. Newsletters like IndieHackers, Hackerpreneur, Bootstrappers.io, and The SaaS Bootstrapper built loyal audiences by curating genuine value, not by optimizing funnels. The pattern: one person sharing real knowledge on a consistent schedule.

## Subject Line Formulas

The subject line determines whether your email gets opened. Target 40%+ open rate:

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Curiosity gap | "The strategy nobody talks about" |
| Specific number | "5 tools I use every morning" |
| Direct benefit | "Double your output this week" |
| Contrarian | "Stop setting goals" |
| Personal | "I almost quit last Tuesday" |
| Question | "Are you making this mistake?" |

**Rules:** Keep under 50 characters. No ALL CAPS. No spam trigger words (free, guarantee, act now). Preview text is your second headline. Use it.

## Email Structure

Every issue should follow a predictable structure readers can scan:

1. **Hook** (1-2 sentences) -- Why this issue matters right now
2. **Main content** (1-3 sections) -- The insight, framework, or story
3. **Actionable takeaway** -- One specific thing the reader can do today
4. **CTA** -- Reply, share, check out a resource, or upgrade
5. **Sign-off** -- Personal, consistent, human

**Formatting rules:**
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
- Bold key phrases for scanners
- Use headers to break sections
- One link per section maximum. Too many links reduce clicks on all of them
- Write at an 8th-grade reading level

## Welcome Sequence

The welcome sequence sets expectations and builds trust. Modeled on how top indie newsletters onboard subscribers:

| Email | Timing | Content |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Welcome | Immediate | Who you are, what to expect, frequency, best past issue link |
| Value bomb | Day 2 | Your single best piece of content. Prove the subscription is worth it |
| Story | Day 4 | Why you started this newsletter. Build personal connection |
| Engagement | Day 7 | Ask a question. "What's your biggest challenge with X?" |
| Best-of | Day 10 | Curated top 3 past issues. Catch them up |

## Growth Tactics

Indie makers who grew newsletters to thousands of subscribers share these proven tactics:

| Tactic | Effort | Impact |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Cross-promotions with similar newsletters | Medium | High |
| Lead magnet (PDF, template, checklist) | Medium | High |
| Referral program (SparkLoop, built-in) | Low | Medium |
| Social media call-to-action on every post | Low | Medium |
| Guest posts in other newsletters | High | High |
| Gated content (free archives behind signup) | Low | Medium |
| Podcast appearances with CTA | High | High |

## Monetization

| Model | When to Start | Revenue Potential |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| Sponsorships | 1,000+ subscribers | $25-50 per 1K subscribers per issue |
| Paid tier | 5,000+ free subscribers | 5-10% conversion at $5-15/month |
| Digital products | Any size | Unlimited, but requires product creation |
| Affiliate links | Any size | Low per-click, scales with trust |
| Community access | 500+ engaged subscribers | $10-30/month for premium community |

**Sponsorship pricing formula:** $25-50 CPM (cost per 1,000 subscribers) per placement. Offer primary (top of email) and secondary (mid or bottom) placements at different rates.

## Deliverability

If your emails land in spam, nothing else matters:

- Authenticate your domain (SPF, DKIM, DMARC records)
- Use a custom sending domain, not the platform's shared domain
- Clean your list quarterly. Remove subscribers who haven't opened in 90 days
- Never buy email lists. Ever
- Include a plain-text version of every email
- Keep image-to-text ratio low (mostly text)
- Add "Reply to confirm" in your welcome email. Replies signal to email providers you're legitimate

## Key Rules

- Send on a consistent schedule. Readers should know when to expect you
- Every email must provide standalone value. No "just checking in" issues
- If you have nothing valuable to say this week, skip the issue. Trust is worth more than consistency
- Think of your newsletter as a micro-SaaS: measure, iterate, and treat subscribers as customers
