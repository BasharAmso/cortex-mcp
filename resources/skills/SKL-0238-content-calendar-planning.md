## Core Concepts

Ghost's editorial workflow demonstrates that sustainable publishing comes from systems, not motivation. The same principle applies to personal brand content: consistency beats intensity. A realistic calendar you actually follow outperforms an ambitious one you abandon in week three.

### Posting Cadence by Platform

Start with a sustainable minimum, then increase:

| Platform | Minimum | Growth Mode | Format Mix |
|----------|---------|-------------|------------|
| LinkedIn | 3x/week | 5x/week | Text posts, carousels, articles |
| Twitter/X | 5x/week | 2-3x/day | Tweets, threads, replies |
| Newsletter | 1x/week | 2x/week | Long-form, curated links |
| Blog | 2x/month | 1x/week | Pillar articles, tutorials |

### Weekly Theme Structure

Assign content themes to days to eliminate decision fatigue:

- **Monday** — Industry insight or trend analysis
- **Tuesday** — Personal story or lesson learned
- **Wednesday** — Tactical how-to or framework
- **Thursday** — Engagement post (question, poll, hot take)
- **Friday** — Curated resource or shout-out to others

Themes are guidelines, not rules. Swap when timely topics arise.

### Batch Creation Workflow

Batch creation is the highest-leverage habit for consistent publishing:

1. **Capture** (ongoing) — Save ideas to a running list throughout the week. Screenshots, quotes, conversations, frustrations are all raw material.
2. **Draft** (one session, 60-90 minutes) — Write 5-7 rough drafts in a single sitting. Do not edit during drafting.
3. **Edit** (separate session, 30-45 minutes) — Tighten each draft. Cut the first paragraph of most posts; the real hook is usually in paragraph two.
4. **Schedule** (15 minutes) — Load posts into your scheduling tool. Space them according to your cadence.
5. **Engage** (daily, 15 minutes) — Reply to comments on your posts and comment on others' posts. This is not optional; engagement drives reach.

### Calendar Planning Template

For each week, capture:

| Day | Theme | Topic | Format | Status |
|-----|-------|-------|--------|--------|
| Mon | Insight | AI adoption gaps in enterprise | Text post | Drafted |
| Tue | Story | My first failed product launch | Carousel | Scheduled |
| Wed | How-to | 3-step framework for X | Text post | Idea |

### Sustainability Rules

- Never queue more than 2 weeks ahead (content goes stale)
- Keep a 10-item idea backlog minimum as buffer
- If you miss a day, skip it rather than double-posting
- Review analytics monthly; double down on what resonates

## Key Takeaways

- Consistency matters more than volume; start with a sustainable minimum
- Assign themes to days to eliminate daily "what should I post" decisions
- Batch creation in dedicated sessions is more efficient than writing daily
- Maintain a running idea capture list so you never start from zero
- Review and adjust monthly based on what your audience actually engages with
