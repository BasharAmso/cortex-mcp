# Personal Branding

Define who you are, who you serve, and why they should pay attention. Personal branding is not self-promotion. It is a promise about the value people get by following you.

## Brand Promise Framework

Your brand promise answers three questions:

1. **Who do you help?** (specific audience, not "everyone")
2. **What do you help them do?** (tangible outcome)
3. **How are you different?** (your unique lens or method)

Template: "I help [audience] [achieve outcome] through [your unique approach]."

## Bio Optimization

Write platform-specific bios, not one-size-fits-all:

| Platform | Priority | Format |
|----------|----------|--------|
| LinkedIn | Credibility + outcomes | Professional headline formula: [Role] helping [audience] [outcome]. [Proof point]. |
| Twitter/X | Personality + hook | Short, punchy. Lead with what you do, end with personality. |
| Newsletter | Value proposition | What subscribers get + frequency + social proof. |
| Personal site | Full story | Brand story + best work + clear CTA. |

Rules: First-person voice. No jargon. Include one concrete proof point. Update quarterly.

## Visual Consistency

- **Profile photo:** Same headshot across all platforms. Professional but approachable.
- **Color palette:** Pick 2-3 brand colors. Use them in banners, graphics, and templates.
- **Typography:** One font for headers, one for body. Use consistently in carousels and graphics.
- **Banner image:** Reinforce brand promise visually. Update when positioning shifts.

## Brand Story Framework

Structure your origin story in four beats:

1. **Before:** Where you started, what you struggled with
2. **Turning point:** The moment everything changed
3. **Method:** What you learned and how you work now
4. **Invitation:** How others can benefit from your journey

Keep it under 200 words. Authenticity beats polish every time.

## Positioning Statement

Fill in: "Unlike [alternative], I [differentiator] because [reason/experience]."

This is internal. It guides content decisions but you never post it verbatim.

## Common Mistakes

- Trying to appeal to everyone (dilutes the brand)
- Copying another creator's voice instead of finding your own
- Inconsistent posting that breaks trust with the audience
- Leading with credentials instead of outcomes
- Changing your positioning every month before it has time to work
- Overthinking the brand before creating any content (start messy, refine later)
